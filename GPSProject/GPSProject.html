Atoms

All atoms have a mass; the Relative Atomic Mass, or 'mass number'; the total mass of an atom in comparison to 1/12 of the mass of an atom of carbon-12.

Therefore, relative atomic mass = Proton mass + Neutron mass + Electron mass, where:

  • Proton mass = 1

  • Neutron mass = 1

  • Electron mass = 1/1836

Since the mass of the electron is so small, at GCSE level, we ignore it. So at GCSE level, the

Mass number = Proton mass + Neutron mass

Oxygen has 8 protons, and 8 neutrons. Therefore, the mass number = 8 + 8 = 16.
Sodium has 11 protons, and 12 neutrons. Therefore, the mass number = 11 + 12 = 23.
**NOTE - the number of protons and neutrons can be different as in sodium!
At GCSE level, we only need to know about the first 20 elements of the periodic table; up to Calcium.

Electronic Configuration

Electrons fill up the energy levels, or 'shells' starting on the innermost shell first - the lowest energy level.

Each shell can only contain a certain number of electrons, at GCSE level;

  • 1st shell - 2 electrons

  • 2nd shell - 8 electrons

  • 3rd shell - 8 electrons

So for example, an atom of carbon contains 6 protons - and therefore 6 electrons. Remembering the 1st shell only takes 2 electrons, so the remaining 4 electrons will have to sit in the second shell. So an atom of carbon would look like this:

We can write the electron configuration for Carbon as [2,4]

An atom of Sodium contains 11 electrons, so again the 1st shell takes 2 electrons, the 2nd shell takes 8 electrons, so the remaining electron sits in the third shell as seen:

Similarly, an atom of Calcium contains 20 electrons, so the electron configuration would be [2,8,8,2].

Periodic Table

Atoms prefer to have a full outer shell - like those in Group 0 - noble gases e.g. Argon [2,8,8]. When atoms do not have a full outer shell, it makes them want to react so that they can achieve a full outer shell. The number of atoms in the outermost shell will decide whether the atom will prefer to lose electrons to achieve a full outer shell, or gain electrons to achieve a full outer shell.

For example, Potassium [2,8,8,1] has 1 electron in its out shell. It is much easier for potassium to lose 1 electron and become [2,8,8]+ than it is for potassium to gain 7 electrons to become [2,8,8,8]. Therefore potassium will lose an electron to achieve a full outer shell.

Chlorine [2,8,7] would find it much easier to gain 1 electron than lose 7, so chlorine is likely to react to become [2,8,8].

So why are some elements more reactive than others?

One reason, is that for example, oxygen [2,6] needs to gain 2 electrons to achieve a full outer shell, while chlorine [2,8,7] only needs to gain 1 electron to achieve its full outer shell. This means Chlorine is more likely to achieve its full outer shell first, and is therefore more reactive.

Ions

When an atom gains, or loses an electron, an ion is formed.

For example, when Chlorine gains an electron and achieves a full outer shell, it has 17 protons in the nucleus (as before) but now has 18 electrons. This means it has a positive charge of +17 and a negative charge of -18. 17-18 = -1. So the overall charge of this new atom of chlorine is -1. We call this a negative ion, this one in particular is [2,8,8]- or Cl-

Similarly, if Calcium [2,8,8,2] lost 2 of its electrons, it would have 20 protons and 18 electrons. This means it would have an overall charge of +20-18 = +2, so would be [2,8,8]2+ or Ca2+
